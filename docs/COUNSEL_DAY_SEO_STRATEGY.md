# Counsel.day SEO Strategy

> Organic search positioning, keyword research findings, and content architecture for counsel.day.
> Source data: Google Ads Keyword Planner pulls (May 2026), 2,253 keywords analysed plus a follow-up "missing phrases" pull.
> Last updated: May 12, 2026.

---

## Executive summary

1. The single highest-volume keyword Counsel.day can credibly own is **`indecisive`** at 100,000 to 1,000,000 searches per month globally, Low competition, $0.07 to $0.52 CPC. This is the front door of the entire category.
2. The market's centre of gravity is **relationships and couples**, not abstract "decision making". Roughly 60% of relevant volume sits in couples/relationship framing.
3. AI-relationship-coach terms are exploding (+900% to +9,900% YoY). Counsel.day sits in the exact intersection of "AI + couples + decision support" where the wind is blowing.
4. The entire vertical is Low competition. Of around 300 keywords analysed, only one is High competition. Nobody is doing serious SEO in this category yet.
5. Realistic addressable organic market: **300,000 to 1,000,000+ searches per month** globally for terms Counsel.day can credibly rank for. This is a content-engine play, not a single-page play.

### Conversion economics

At maturity, assuming 5% CTR for top-3 SERP positions and 2% conversion to signup, a 400,000-searches-per-month addressable market yields roughly **400 signups per month from organic alone**. At a $5 to $10 USD/month Pro tier with normal conversion to paid, that's a defensible indie SaaS revenue line before any paid acquisition.

## The four cornerstone keywords

These four pages, executed well, anchor the entire SEO strategy. Build these first, internally link them tightly, make them the best content on the internet for each phrase.

| Keyword | Volume/mo | Competition | YoY | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `indecisive` | 100K to 1M | Low | 0% | The diagnosis, the front door |
| `analysis paralysis` | 10K to 100K | Low | 0% | The textbook term |
| `decision paralysis` | 10K to 100K | Low | 0% | Same energy, separate page |
| `can't decide` | 10K to 100K | Low | **+900%** | The conversational variant, exploding |

**Why these four**: they're the four ways real people describe the problem Counsel.day solves. They're all Low competition. They cover both the formal psychological framing ("analysis paralysis") and the casual lived-experience framing ("can't decide", "indecisive"). Combined they could realistically reach 250,000+ searches per month.

## Genuine surprises

### Surprise 1: "indecisive" dominates everything

The single keyword `indecisive` outweighs the rest of the relationship-focused volume put together. My initial brief positioned Counsel.day around couples and life decisions. The data says the front door should actually be the emotional state of indecision, with relationships and life decisions as the specific use cases below it.

### Surprise 2: Name generators are a hidden 300K/month market

Six keywords at 50K searches/month each. None of this is what Counsel.day is built for, but the underlying mechanic (two people deciding on a name together) is exactly what Counsel.day does. Massive top-of-funnel side door.

### Surprise 3: "Pros and cons list" is how people actually describe Counsel.day

Five separate "pros and cons list" keywords. This is the lay-language version of what Counsel.day does. Worth using the phrase prominently in copy, even if "decision journal" or "decision support" is the technical positioning.

### Surprise 4: Country-specific relocation decisions are queries

`should we move to australia`, `should we move to canada`, `should we move to new zealand`. Low individual volume but specific high-stakes decisions. Marketing angle: "Counsel.day for emigration decisions" with localised landing pages.

### Surprise 5: "Decision fatigue relationship" is a real search

Someone, somewhere, is typing that into Google. The problem statement is independently validated.

### Surprise 6: Couples therapy is cooling

`couples therapy app` at 5,000/mo searches but -90% YoY. The post-COVID surge in therapy-as-app is reversing. Counsel.day should NOT position itself as therapy. The growth is in coaching, tracking, and tools.

### Surprise 7: Pet naming is bigger than expected

Variants of "what should I name my [pet]" cover dog, cat, kitten, puppy, bird, fish, rabbit, turtle, horse. Several show +900% YoY. Cumulative volume in the thousands per month. A "Counsel.day for pets" angle is silly but real.

## Competition landscape

Of approximately 300 keywords with competition data:

| Level | Count | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Low | ~220 | Wide open, content-engine SEO wins |
| Medium | ~32 | Mostly app-install categories |
| High | ~8 | A handful of saturated terms to either avoid or beat with deep content |

### High-competition terms (the few to be careful with)

- `couples therapy worksheet`
- `marriage conversation starters`
- `couples counselling app`
- `marriage counselling app`
- `shared journal for couples`
- `journal for two`
- `brand name chooser`
- `deciding whether to separate`
- `what should we name our son`

For these, do not try to outrank with thin pages. Either skip, or commit to flagship-quality content (3,000+ words, multimedia, tools, the works).

### Strategic read

This is the kind of competition profile that calculate.co.nz-style execution (lots of high-quality calculator/tool pages, internal linking, schema markup, fast site) would dominate. Most direct competitors in the space are app-install businesses spending on paid acquisition, not SEO. Their organic footprint is small.

### Page template requirements

Every SEO page should include:

1. **H1 with exact-match keyword** (or close variant)
2. **Introduction (150 to 250 words)** that defines the problem in the user's own words
3. **An embedded Counsel.day tool** specific to the page (calculator, framework, journal entry, pros-cons list, etc)
4. **Educational content (800 to 1,500 words)** that genuinely helps even without signup
5. **Real examples and scenarios** specific to the keyword
6. **FAQ section** with schema.org/FAQPage markup
7. **Internal links** to 5 to 10 related pages in the same cluster
8. **CTA to signup** placed at 30%, 60%, and 90% scroll depth
9. **Original meta description** under 155 chars

### Schema markup priorities

- `SoftwareApplication` schema on the homepage
- `FAQPage` schema on every long-tail page
- `HowTo` schema on every "how to" page
- `Article` schema on educational content

## Positioning recommendations

### What to put on the homepage above the fold

The data says the headline should NOT be "daily decision tracking" or "decision journal" alone. The phrase real people use is closer to:

> **"Can't decide? Counsel.day helps you and your partner make better decisions, together."**

Or:

> **"For the decisions you can't stop overthinking."**

Both lead with the emotional state ("can't decide", "overthinking") that has the highest search volume and most relatable framing.

### What to call the AI Pro feature

Not "AI Insights". Brand it explicitly as one of:

- **AI Decision Coach** (broadest, claims `ai life coach app` and `ai decision making app`)
- **AI Relationship Coach** (rides the +9,900% YoY wave on `relationship coach app`)

Probably both, with `/ai/decision-coach` as the page and `/ai/relationship-coach` as a parallel page targeting the couples vertical.

### What words to use in copy throughout the site

Lead with these phrases (highest search volume, most relatable):

- indecisive
- can't decide
- analysis paralysis
- decision paralysis
- overthinking
- feeling stuck
- torn between
- big life decisions
- hard decisions
- decisions you can't stop thinking about

Use sparingly or behind context:

- decision journal
- decision support
- decision matrix
- AI decision coach

Avoid entirely:

- therapy (declining, wrong framing)
- counselling (high competition, wrong framing)
- couples therapy (declining)

## Risks and watch-outs

1. **"Should we" content can read as clickbait**. Each page needs to be genuinely useful, not a thin landing page funnel. Otherwise Google demotes the whole site under the Helpful Content algorithm.

2. **Mental-health adjacency**. Some of these queries (`should i end my marriage`, `should we divorce`, `feeling stuck in life`) overlap with people in genuine distress. The content has to handle that with care, with crisis-resource footers where appropriate. This is also a YMYL (Your Money Your Life) zone where Google demands E-E-A-T signals (real author, credentials, citations).

3. **AI content detection**. If the long tail pages are written by AI without human review, Google's spam detection will catch and demote. Every page needs human editorial pass, original examples, original framing.

4. **Don't chase declining queries as flagships**. Pages for `couples therapy app` etc are fine to have but should not be the strategic centre. The market is moving away from therapy framing.

5. **Domain age penalty**. counsel.day is a new domain. Expect 6 to 12 months before serious rankings. Plan content accordingly.
