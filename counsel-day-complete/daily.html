THE DAILY COUNSEL · A REFLECTION JOURNAL WITH WEEKLY ANALYSIS

Reflect on today. See the pattern on Monday.

A standalone reflection journal. Type or speak for a minute about today · what worked, what dragged, what surprised you. The entry seals for seven days; you cannot re-read it. Each Monday morning an analysis arrives in your inbox: three to five things that kept working, one or two that kept straining, the throughline of the week, and one specific question for the days ahead.

A journal that reads itself back to you.

Most journaling apps put the load on you · pick a mood, score the day, fill the streak. The Daily Counsel puts the load on the analysis. You write the day, in your own words, and on Monday morning a verdict ships in the editorial voice naming what kept working, what kept straining, the throughline of the week, and one question worth carrying forward.

Why the seven-day seal. If you can re-read tonight's entry tomorrow morning you edit it. The seal removes that option · you write to the future you, not to the immediate one, and the verdict reads what you actually meant rather than what you re-wrote.

Why the verdict leads with positives. Noticing what's working is the under-trained half of reflection. The analysis names three to five things that kept landing as alive in your week before it names the one or two things that kept landing as a strain. The order is the practice.

No streaks. No scores. No mood emoji. No nudging notifications. Free for the daily entry, the seven-day seal, and the weekly Monday verdict · forever. Pro ($4.99 USD/month) adds a monthly themed deep-dive verdict that reads the past four weekly verdicts and names what the month was actually about.
