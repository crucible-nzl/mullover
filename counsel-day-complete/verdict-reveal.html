Both lines rose; they did not match.

James opened at Lean No and closed at Strong Yes; Alexandra opened at No and closed at Lean Yes. The slopes are different, the destinations are close. A verdict made of two converging slopes is structurally different from a verdict made of two flat lines · it tells you the decision is durable, that both of you moved with reason.

File · Synthesis

What you were actually arguing about.

Synthesis paragraph · day 30

The surface question was whether to move into the city for school. The axis underneath, as your notes consistently revealed, was a different question: what does each of you owe the village you have built, and what does each of you owe the child you are raising inside it? James moved first because the curriculum question was decisive for him; Alexandra moved second, more slowly, because the loss of the village was a real cost that the curriculum did not erase. The verdict is a yes, with a quiet grief.

File · The one specific question

What to talk about tonight.

"Alexandra, what do you want to keep from the village, deliberately, even after the move? James, what specifically do you want to make sure he carries with him from the place he was born?"
