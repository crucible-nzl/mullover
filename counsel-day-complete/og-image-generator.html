Export to /og-image.png

This canvas is exactly 1200 × 630 at 1× device-pixel-ratio. Three ways to get the PNG out of it:

  1. Headless (recommended) · run npx playwright screenshot http://localhost:8000/og-image-generator.html --selector="#og-canvas" --device-scale-factor=2 og-image.png from the repo root with a local server. Produces a sharp 2400×1260 PNG; save as og-image.png at the site root.
  2. Browser DevTools · in Chrome / Edge, open this page, right-click <div id="og-canvas"> in the inspector, choose Capture node screenshot. Saves at 1200×630 1×.
  3. macOS Preview · take a window screenshot with Cmd+Shift+4, drag-select the canvas, drop the resulting PNG into the repo at counsel-day-complete/og-image.png. Quickest; lower fidelity than 1.

Once og-image.png exists at the canonical URL, every page's og:image meta tag (already referenced on all 41 HTML files) will resolve and social cards will render correctly across Facebook, LinkedIn, X, Slack, iMessage, Discord. Until then they fall back to plain-text previews.

Editing the image · change copy in .og-pitch on this page (eyebrow / h1 / dek), or swap the illustration SVG inside .og-illus. Refresh; re-export. The wordmark and brand mark are baked in via the same logo SVG used in every page's masthead, so they match exactly.

Twitter card variants · X uses 1200×675 for summary_large_image (very close to 1200×630, no separate render needed). Pinterest prefers 1200×1500 (vertical) · for that one, swap the grid to a stacked layout if it ever becomes a meaningful traffic source.
